If the results of the updated valuation demonstrate (or if Figure receives notice via another channel) that the value of the borrower‚Äôs property has significantly declined below it‚Äôs original value determined at origination for purposes of the HELOC, Figure will suspend additional draws from the borrower‚Äôs line of credit.

To qualify as a significant decrease in the property value, the initial difference between the credit limit and the available equity (based on the property‚Äôs appraised value for purposes of the plan) must be reduced by at least 85%. This means the equity in the home had to have been reduced by at least 85%, not the direct value of the home. Figure will send an Additional Draw Suspension Notice to the borrower no later than three (3) business days from the date of suspension, and the borrower will not be able to take out an additional draw for 60 days.

- The borrower will receive an Additional Draw Suspension Notice (see example below), which will include the most recent AVM amount.
- The borrower is unable to dispute the updated valuation, but their ability to request additional draws will be automatically reinstated 60 days after the requested date.
- Future draws are not guaranteed and are subject to further AVMs and value reviews.
- This possibility is outlined in the HELOC agreement titled: Suspending Additional Draws; Reducing Credit Limit and Available Credit.

# **Servicing - Forbearance**

## What is Forbearance?

- If you‚Äôre experiencing income loss or financial hardship you may be eligible for payment assistance.

## How does your forbearance program work?

- Figure offers payment forbearance in 3 mo. increments for a maximum of 12 months over the life of the line, for those who are eligible. For COVID-related hardship the maximum is 18 months. If approved for forbearance, you will not be required to make your monthly payments for 3 months, but interest will continue to accrue during that time.
    - For borrowers in MA and MD, amounts that accrue in excess of would have accrued had the Deferred Payments been made will be credited to the line at the end of the forbearance period.
- If you would like to continue making payments during the forbearance period, you are free to do so at your option.
- At the end of the 3-month period, the line will be re-amortized across the remaining term, keeping the same maturity date. This means the total amount due at that time will be spread out evenly across the remaining number of months left on the line, which may result in your monthly payment amount going up. Interest will not be calculated on the interest that accrued during the forbearance period.
- The next payment will not be due until the regularly scheduled due date after the 3 month forbearance period has ended.
- You will not be able to make any additional draws from the account during the forbearance period and until 3 consecutive payments have been made after the forbearance period ends.

We require for you to complete a Loan Assistance Agreement (LAA). The form must be signed, dated and returned back to [myaccount@figure.com](mailto:myaccount@figure.com).
